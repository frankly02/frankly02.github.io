World Scholars Cup

I participated in the Worlds Scholars Cup (WSC) as a member of the club in high school. The WSC is an annual multi-round competition with a variety of subjects to compete as teams of three. It features three rounds: regional rounds in individual cities, global rounds that encompass qualified competitors from regional rounds all around the world, and a final round at Yale University. Some subjects it touches on include: Debate, Literature, Science, and Social Studies.

In the two years I was a member of the club, I attended a regional round in Toronto, and a global round in Manila, Philippines. The competition itself was incredibly fun, as the topics chosen for the competition were all very interesting to discuss with my teammates. Regardless of the outcome of the competition, I would always be able to have fun trying my best with my peers. I especially had fun during the global round in Manila I participated in, as I got to both travel with my friends and compete against other students from different countries; something I thought I would never have an opportunity to experience. Overall, World Scholars Cup was an exciting experience in my life, and is something I wish I could continue participating in.
